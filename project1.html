HealthTrack Redesign

A comprehensive redesign of a health tracking application focusing on accessibility, user engagement, and data visualization. The project involved extensive user research, iterative prototyping, and usability testing with 50+ participants.

User Research Prototyping Usability Testing Design System
Hero Image

The Challenge

HealthTrack's existing interface suffered from poor information hierarchy and overwhelming data displays. Users struggled to understand their health metrics and felt demotivated by the complex navigation structure.

Key pain points identified:

  • Cognitive overload from dense information architecture
  • Lack of clear visual hierarchy
  • Poor accessibility for colorblind users
  • Confusing onboarding experience

The Approach

Conducted extensive user research including 25 in-depth interviews and diary studies with existing users. Created personas representing three distinct user segments and mapped their journeys to identify critical touchpoints.

Research insights:

  • Users wanted quick glanceable summaries
  • Motivation came from progress visualization
  • Customization was key to sustained engagement
  • Social features needed opt-in privacy controls

Design Process

01 — Research & Discovery

Started with competitive analysis of 8 health apps, followed by user interviews and contextual inquiry sessions. Created affinity diagrams to synthesize findings and identify common patterns across user behaviors.

Developed three primary personas: The Casual Tracker, The Data Enthusiast, and The Recovery-Focused Athlete. Each persona had distinct needs and interaction patterns that informed design decisions.

Research Artifacts

02 — Information Architecture

Restructured the navigation from a complex 5-tab system to a streamlined 3-tab structure with contextual actions. Conducted card sorting sessions with 15 participants to validate the new hierarchy.

Created detailed user flows for key tasks: logging activities, viewing progress, and setting goals. Each flow was tested and refined through multiple iterations.

IA Diagrams

03 — Wireframing & Prototyping

Developed low-fidelity wireframes focusing on layout and content priority. Progressed to mid-fidelity prototypes in Figma, incorporating micro-interactions and transitions to simulate the final experience.

Built a high-fidelity interactive prototype with 42 screens and comprehensive component library. All components were designed with accessibility guidelines (WCAG 2.1 AA) in mind.

Wireframes

04 — Usability Testing

Conducted three rounds of usability testing with 50+ participants. Each session included task-based scenarios and follow-up interviews. Analyzed results using System Usability Scale (SUS) scoring.

Key improvements from testing: simplified goal-setting flow (task completion +32%), redesigned data visualization (comprehension +48%), and refined onboarding (drop-off -41%).

The redesign successfully addressed all major usability issues identified in initial research. User retention improved by 47% in the first three months post-launch, and the app achieved its highest-ever App Store ratings.

The design system created for this project has since been adopted across the company's other health-focused products, ensuring consistency and reducing design debt.
